A Cleaning Method

Clean it well, room by room

I've sorted the home into zones by how much rigour each one asks for — from the sanitising work of the bathroom to the light touch of a balcony. Open any space for the products I reach for, the order I work in, routines to keep it up, and the cautions I never ignore.

Bathroom — highest rigour, sanitising Kitchen — high rigour, food-safe Living & storage — moderate, dust & textiles Outdoor & entry — lighter, transitional Appliances — periodic deep service

No spaces in this zone.

How I use this guide. Every room follows the same method: I work top to bottom and cleanest to dirtiest, and on each surface I dust or vacuum first, then wash, then sanitise where it's warranted — a disinfectant only bites on an already-clean surface. The colours are more than a label — I assign a cloth colour to each zone and never let them cross, which is the simplest way I know to keep the dirtiest surfaces from touching the food-safe ones. My kit is deliberately bleach-free, so the classic chlorine-gas reactions don't apply; the one rule that does is to keep my acids — Windex-vinegar, citric acid, Bar Keeper's Friend — away from the hydrogen-peroxide Bio Stain. When in doubt, I use one product, rinse or let it dry, and only then reach for the next.

    Tools & cloths

      On the colour-coded cloths. A separate microfiber colour for each zone — red for the bathroom, blue for the kitchen, yellow for living and storage, green for outdoor and entry — is a professional habit worth borrowing. It stops a cloth that wiped the toilet from ever meeting a kitchen counter. I keep each colour to its zone, launder them separately, and replace them well before they fray.

The Card

Everything that actually matters, on one page. The full reference is for looking things up, not for doing the work.

Daily

10 min · evening
If today is a hard day

Do the first item only. Kitchen closed, nothing soaking. That is a complete day. It is not a reduced version of the protocol — it is the protocol on a low-energy day, and skipping the rest costs you nothing.

Weekly

60 min · Saturday

Order matters, the timings don't. Bedroom, bathroom, living, balcony, kitchen, entry, floors. Top to bottom in each room. One cloth colour per room.

If the hour runs out

Stop anyway. The order above is deliberate — it puts the highest-consequence rooms where you still have energy, so whatever gets dropped is whatever matters least. Finishing late is not better than finishing short.

Monthly

2 h · first Saturday

Laundry goes on first so it runs while you work. Split across the day if that's easier — nothing here has to happen in one sitting.

The eight things that cause permanent damage

Everything else in the reference is preference or efficiency. These are the ones that cannot be undone. If you remember nothing else, remember these.

  • No standing water or steam on the laminate. The core swells and it is unfixable.
  • No abrasives on the fibreglass tub. No Bar Keeper's Friend, no powders, no green pads.
  • No Bar Keeper's Friend or vinegar on the granite. Dulls the polish, strips the sealer.
  • No alcohol inside the fridge or on the toilet seat. It cracks both plastics.
  • No bleach on stainless. You don't own bleach. Keep it that way.
  • No liquid on any book. None, ever.
  • No percarbonate on the wooden bench. It bleaches and raises the grain.
  • Never mix products. One product per surface, rinse between.

What to use, in one line each

  • Dawn — everything, first. This is 80% of all cleaning.
  • Bio Stain — the disinfectant. Wet zones. One minute wet, then wipe.
  • 70% alcohol — dry touchpoints and electronics. On the cloth, never sprayed at a device.
  • Bar Keeper's Friend — stainless sink and porcelain only.
  • Windex Vinegar — glass and mirrors only.
  • Citric acid — descaling only. Kettle, shower head, appliance cycles.
  • Percarbonate — soaking only. Balcony floor, tub scum, grout.

Cloth colours: blue kitchen, red bathroom, green bedroom and living, green outdoor set for the balcony, white flat-weave for glass and screens. Hot wash, never fabric softener.

What "clean enough" means here: no visible soil in the rooms you addressed, surfaces dry, cloths in the laundry. That is the finish line. Reaching it and stopping is the protocol working correctly, not a compromise.

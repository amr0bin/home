Clinical Reset

A three-phase cleaning and sanitization protocol for a shared living and working space, structured on the infection-prevention logic used in clinical environments: interrupt transmission at the points that carry it, remove organic matter before disinfecting, and keep the process repeatable at a sustainable effort level.

    Section 2

    Standing principles

    These apply to all three phases and are stated once. Where a phase step is ambiguous, the principle governs.

    Clean, disinfect, sterilize

    Cleaning removes soil and organic matter through detergent and friction. Disinfection reduces surviving microorganisms on an already-clean surface. Sterilization is a clinical process and is not indicated in a domestic setting.

    Cleaning always precedes disinfection. Disinfectant applied over organic matter is largely inactivated by it.

    Order of operations

    • Top to bottom — surfaces before floors
    • Dry to wet — dust before wiping
    • Clean to contained — cleanest zone first, sanitary areas last
    • Low-touch before high-touch within a zone

    Zoning

    • Clean: kitchen, sleeping area, studio
    • Transition: entry, balcony
    • Contained: bathroom, waste, floor edges

    Equipment does not move from a contained zone to a clean zone without reprocessing.

    One cloth, one zone

    Assign cloths by colour and keep the assignment fixed. A cloth is retired to laundry at the end of its zone rather than rinsed and reused. Bathroom brushes remain in the bathroom.

    Contact time

    A disinfectant is effective only while the surface remains visibly wet for the interval stated on its label, typically one to five minutes. Wipe after that interval has elapsed, not before. The dial in section 3 is provided for this purpose.

    Incompatible combinations

    Do not combine bleach with ammonia or with any acid, including citric acid. Do not combine hydrogen peroxide with an acid. Store products in their original containers or in clearly labelled, dated decant bottles, and ventilate the area during use.

    Balcony containment

    The balcony has no drain, and anything applied to it is above a neighbouring space. Introduce only the volume of solution a bucket can take back, wring over the bucket rather than the edge, and carry wastewater indoors for disposal. Nothing is hosed, flooded or swept over the railing.

    Droppings and dried soil

    Dried organic deposits are moistened before removal. Dry brushing or sweeping aerosolizes the material and moves it into the breathing zone. Wet, cover, lift, bag, then disinfect and observe the dwell interval.

    Section 4

    Completion criteria

    A phase is complete when the following conditions are met. Additional work beyond them is outside the protocol.

    Air

    Odour-neutral. No detectable residue from cleaning agents.

    Surfaces

    Dry to the touch, not tacky. No visible soil in the zones addressed by the phase.

    Equipment

    Cloths in laundry, brushes rinsed and drying, decant bottles closed and labelled. Balcony wastewater disposed of indoors.

    Reservoirs

    No visible accumulation in the concealed areas scheduled for the current phase. Textiles laundered and returned, curtains rehung.

    Handover

    The next session has a defined starting point, recorded in one line.

    Effort

    The allotted interval has elapsed. The protocol treats stopping on time as adherence, not as an incomplete result.

    Notes

    This protocol adapts clinical infection-prevention principles — cleaning before disinfection, zoning, single-zone equipment, observed contact time, and defined completion criteria — to a domestic environment. It is not a substitute for regulated healthcare cleaning standards, and it does not address settings in which clinical care is delivered. Product label instructions and safety data sheets govern all agent use.
